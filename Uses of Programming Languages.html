Most programming languages consist of the computer instructions. There are programmable machines that use a set of specific instructions instead of programming languages generally. The early ones preceded the invention of the digital computer, the first was possibly the automatic flute player mentioned by the Musa brothers in Baghdad during the Islamic Golden Age in the 9th century. Since the early 1800s programs have been used to control the actions of machines such as Jacquard looms, music boxes and player pianos.
